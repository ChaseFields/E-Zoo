Africa!

Lion

Lion

Lions are the second largest big cat species in the world, and are very social compared to other cats, like tigers. They can run up to 50 miles per hour and their roar can be heard up to five miles away. Male lions are very recognizable due to the large furry main that wraps around their head and neck area.

Elephant

Elephant

The largest land animal in the world, elephans live in families called herds and are known for their large trunks, ears, and tusks. They spend 12 to 18 hours per day eating! They also love their time in the water! And are often found playing in rivers.

Rhino

Rhino

Though rhinos are found in different parts of the world, the kinds native to africa are called the Black Rhino and the White Rhino. They eat grass and plants and are very recognizable because of the large horn on their nose. In fact, the word "rhinoceros" means "nose horn".

Ostrich

Ostrich

The ostrich is the world's largest bird. It has a long flexible neck and a very small head. It has a big bulky body covered with feathers. Even though it has all these feathers, it still cannot fly! However, it can run up to 70 miles per hour and cover five meters in a single stride!

Giraffe

Giraffe

Known by its very long neck, the giraffe is the world's tallest land animal. They only eat plants. Their long neck and tongue allow them to reach food that is way up in trees. Giraffes are very social and typically travel in groups of up to fifteen giraffes called "towers". Males often hit their necks and heads together to see who is stronger.

Crocodile

Crocodile

Known as the Nile crocodile in Africa, it is the largest and heaviest reptile. An adult male is about 16 feet long and weighs between 500 and 1200 pounds! They are predators, which means they hunt other animals. They are also very good at hiding and sneaking up on their food, which may explain why they have been around for so long!

Antarctica!

Penguin

Penguin

Though they are birds, penguins cannot fly. Instead, they have wing like limbs they use for flippers to help swim. They spend half their time on land and the other half in water. Incredibly, they can spend about 20 minutes under water at a time! They eat other fish and other water animal life, and often huddle together to keep warm.

Orca

Orca

Orcas are black and white 6000 pound hunting machines! They live between fifty and eighty years in the wild. They need alot of food to keep them going. So they may eat around 500 pounds of food per day. They hunt in groups and work together to capture their food. A common way of hunting is to capture a seal by bumping it off the sheet of ice it sits on.

Seal

Seal

Seals are truly made for swimming. They sometimes need to travel hundreds of miles to find food. Amazingly, they can dive very deep underwater and stay there for up to two hours without coming up for air. They have a thick layer of fat called blubber to keep them warm in the very cold environment.

Snow Petrel

Snow Petrel

The snow petrel is one of the few bird species that only live in Antarctica. They eat mainly fish and mollusks, diving quickly to catpure fish swimming near the top of the water. They can fly up to 40 kilometers per hour and live up to twenty years in the wild.

Blue Eyed Shag

Blue Eyed Shag

The Blue Eyed Shag is the only bird in Antarctica to keep a nest the whole year. You might say it likes to stay home! Sailers or sea explorers were happy to see the shag because they knew land was near. To catch fish and other food, they will dive over 25 meters below the water and are able to stay under about four minutes. There can be hundreds of them at a time going fishing together!

Albatross

Wandering Albatross

With a wing span of 10 feet, the Wandering Albatross has the longest wing span of any bird on the planet. Truly, a claim to fame! Why wandering? This bird takes fishing trips of 10 to 20 days and can cover 10,000 kilometers using very little effort. Their huge wingspan allows the wind to carry them rathern than a lot of flapping. Their babies are born large. It only takes 1 year for a new born Albatross to be fully grown.

Asia!

Panda

Panda

The giant panda is found in China. There are less than 1,900 left in the world, which means they are in danger of extinction. It has a very distinct black and white coat of fur with black patchs of fur around its eyes. The main thing they eat is bamboo. And they eat alot! Around 22 pounds a day! An adult male can weigh around 330 pounds. But even so, they are really good climbers, often found eating and relaxing in trees.

Bengal Tiger

Bengal Tiger

The bengal tiger is found mainly in India. These huge cats are carnivores. They eat animals such as wild pigs, antelope, and buffalo. They usually hunt alone at night for food and eat up to 60 pounds of meat a day! Just as no two fingerprints are alike, no two tigers have the same stripes. Each tiger has its own special stripe pattern. Like the panda, we have been losing tigers in large numbers. They are in danger of extinction.

King Cobra

King Cobra

The king Cobra is the longest poisounous snake in the world, growing to between 13 and 18 feet. They live in Southeast Asia, inlcuding parts of India. A single bite can be very deadly. One bite could kill an elephant or up to 20 grown men! It is the only snake that makes a nest for its eggs. The mother will stay with the eggs until they hatch. Its only predator is the mongoose, because it is immune to the venom.

Orangutan

Orangutan

Orangutans are made to climb! They spend a lot of time in trees eating and relaxing. Orangutans sleep in nests high in trees. This makes it hard for leopards or other hunters to get them. Unlike other apes, orangutans don't have much group time. Females usually have a baby or two with her, but males like to be alone. They can be very loud, being heard a few miles away. Sadly, they are also in danger of extincion.

Asian Tapir

Asian Tapir

Found in Malaysia, the asian Tapir is a uniquely shaped mammal. They usually weigh between 500 and 700 pounds, but can weigh over 1000 pounds. They can't see well, but can smell and hear very well! They eat only plants. They move slowly through the forest eating and smelling for other creatures. A tapir can run fast and use its powerful jaws and sharp teeth to defend itself. As a result the Tapir has few predators.

Binturong

Binturong

This 40 pound mammal looks like a cat and bear combo. Even stranger is the Binturong's scent. It smells like buttered popcorn. Yes, buttered popcorn! It spends a lot of time at top of trees, and uses its very long tail to grap limbs and move about. They eat small animals, such as birds. But also, love to eat fruit and insects. They are nocturnal, which means they do most of their moving around at night.

Australia!

Kangaroo

Kangaroo

With short front legs, powerful back legs, huge back feet and a strong tail, kangaroos are made for jumping! Mother kangaroos carry their baby in a belly pouch for about 8 months. After birth, the baby gets in the pouch and stays for about 8 months. They are quite fast, traveling at between 20 to 25 miles an hour usually. But they can get up to 40 miles an hour! Male kangaroos actually box each other to see who is the toughest.

Tasmanian Devil

Tasmanian Devil

Only found on Tazmania, an island off the Australian Coast, the tazmanian devil is about the size of a small dog. It is very fiesty! With very sharp teeth and a strong jaw, it delivers one of the most powerful bites of any mammal on earth. They are carnivores, eating small prey and dead animals. When provoked they will jump at the attacker often spinning in circles, shrieking and howling, much like their famous cartoon character.

Koala

Koala

Though often called "Koala Bears", the koala is not a bear. It is a marcupial. So after birth, the baby continues to grow inside the mother's pouch. They are usually out at night. Most of their water comes from leaves, so they don't drink much water, except during dry times. After about six months, the young koala will come out of its mother's pouch and ride on her back for another six months, until it is ready to climb without help.

Platypus

Platypus

Truly a unique creature! The platypus is a mammal that lays eggs, has a beak like a duck, and a beaver like tail. They close their eyes and ears when diving under water, so their bills are able to detect the electricity given off by prey. When it finds food under water, it stores it in a pouch inside its mouth until it comes back to the surface, only then does it eat. They have venom in their back ankles that can be used on prey.

Box Jellyfish

Box Jellyfish

Unlike other jellyfish that can only float, these guys can swim! Called 'box' because of the box shape of their bell, they are the most poisonous water animal in their world. They have up to 15 tenticles that grow from each corner. The tenticles grow up to 10 feet. Each tenticle has 5,000 stinging cells that fire when detecting a chemical on the surface of their prey.

Great White

Great White

The largest predator fish on the planet is the great white shark. A lot of them live in Australian waters. They have about 300 razor sharp teeth and a powerful bite! They are grey on top but white on bottom. Sea lions, seals, and small whales make up the main diet of adult great whites. Their sense of smell is so strong that they can smell a colony of seals two miles away! And can smell one drop of blood in 26 gallons of water!

Europe!

Reindeer

Reindeer

Unlike every other kind of deer, even female reindeer grow antlers! Like our fingerprints, no two antlers are alike. They live up to 15 years in the wild. Their favorite food is a kind of fungi called 'lichen', which is found in open spaces of land. Reindeer spend a lot of their lives in the snow, so their hooves are good for walking on soft surfaces and their fur traps heat very well.

Eurasian Lynx

Eurasian Lynx

The Eurasian Lynx is the third largest predator in Europe, behind the wolf and brown bear. It is very active early in the morning and right at dark time. But during most of the day and night, it will be found lying in thickets or trees that are safe for hiding. They only eat other animals, from birds to deer.

Eurasian Wolf

Eurasian Wolf

The largest wolf outside of North America, the Eurasian wolf is a very social animal, living and hunting in groups called 'packs'. It also protects its territory aggresively. The leading cause of natural death among the wolf is fighting over territory.Wolves are known for their long distint 'howl'. This howl is to bring the pack together and warn other packs to stay away.

Chamois

Chamois

The chamois is a kind of goat-antelope, perfectly suited for life in the alps mountain ranges and meadows. Their hooves help them to move around on very rocky, slippery surfaces. They have short, mostly straight horns that hook at the top. They announce danger by stomping their feet and whistling, and get away from danger by climbing to places that are hard for other animals to get too.

Wolverine

Wolverine

Wolverines are the largest member of the weasel family. Known to be aggressive, fierce, and tough, they are not afraid to stand up to larger animals. They like to be alone, and are known to travel up to 15 miles in a day for food. They are good climbers and will often follow wolves to eat the leftovers of prey.

Puffin

Puffin

Only about 10 inches tall, the puffin spends most of its time out on the water, resting on waves if not swimming. Its beak color changes with the seasons! It is gray in Winter and bright orange in Spring. They are fast fliers! Flapping their wings about 400 times per minute and flying at 88 kilometers per hour.

North America!

Brown Bear

Brown Bear

Commonly known as the Grizzly, the adult brown bear weighs between 300 and 900 pounds, making it the second largest bear in the world. It loves fish, especially salmon. It fishes with its paw or catches fish in its mouth. But they also eat anything from berries and knuts to deer and other small mammals. In the winter, they find or dig caves and sleep most of the season. Their heart rate slows to about 10 beats per minute. This is called "hibernation."

Beaver

Beaver

The American Beaver is only 1 of 2 living kinds of beaver in the world. Beavers are well known for making dams, which are collections of limbs, leaves, twigs and mud that provide them and other animals with shelter and deep water to hide from predators. Their large front teeth never stop growing. The beaver is constantly chewing on wood, and this keep the teeth from growing out of control.

Moose

Moose

The moose can weigh as much as 1,300 pounds and have the largest antler spread in the world, of up to 6 feet. The antlers along can weigh up to 65 pounds and are used for self defense and fighting, usually over a lady moose! Their diet consists of tall grasses and shrubs, as well as wooded bark and pinecones. They love to swim. In the spring, they will swim through the water looking for food.

Arctic Fox

Arctic Fox

Living throughout the coldest parts of North America, the arctic fox is able live in temperatures below -30 degrees. Their fur gives them the best protection from the cold. They will bury their heads and legs under their body during the most extreme cold. The fox will eat most any small animal it finds. Their sense of smell and hearing is very strong. They can smell prey hiding under snow. So even if prey cannot be seen, they will pounce to capture it.

Gila Monster

Gila Monster

The Gila monster is the only poisonous lizard in the United States. Though it is called a "monster," the Gila usually weighs only a pound or few, but has been known to get to 5 pounds. It is not really dangerous for humans, but its venom kills small rodents, frogs, and birds. Most of their time is spent underground or in rocky shelters. In 2005, a drug made from the Gila's saliva was released that helps people with Diabetes.

Cougar

Cougar

The cougar goes by many names, including panther, mountain lion, and puma. It is a meat eater, usually feeding on deer, mice, rabbits, or other mammals. They can't smell well, but their hearing and vision is unbelievable, making them very good at finding prey. Their jumping abilities are incredible; being able to leap 18 feet from a sitting position. They also hide their prey, coming back to eat over several days. They don't roar, but make a loud scream.

South America!

Sloth

Sloth

The famous tree dwelling mammal featured in Disney's Zootopia, sloths are one of the slowest moving animals on earth. What makes them so slow? Sloths have a 4 part stomach that very slowly digests the tough leaves they eat. It can take up to a month to digest a meal. With all this energy used for digestion, they have little energy to move around. Their tongue can extend to almost a foot long! and is very useful for collecting food.

Jaguar

Jaguar

This big cat is the largest cat in the americas. It is a swift hunter and will hide its prey in trees for safe keeping. Deer, pigs, rodents and anacondas could be on the jag menu. Like tigers they are at home in dense rain forests and enjoy swimming. They live, roam, and hunt alone, only coming together to mate. Its bite is even stronger than a lion. It looks similar to the african leopard, but is larger and has small spots inside the circles of it's fur.

Anaconda

Anaconda

Though a python can grow longer, the Anaconda is the heaviest and thickest snake in the world, weighing up to 550 pounds! It will eat any animal it can catch, from small rodents to deer and capybara. It squeezes its prey, rather than giving them venom. Its flexible jaw and throat muscles allow it to eat animals whole, without chewing. After a large meal, it may not eat for weeks. Unlike many snakes, Anacondas do not lay eggs, but give birth to live young.

Piranha

Piranha

On average, it is only 8 to 10 inches long. But this fish packs a bite! The "red belly piranha" has razor sharp teeth and fierce biting power. Especially in shallow water, these fish swim and hunt in groups of up to 100 called "shoals". These shoals are useful for hunting, but are also important for protection against predators. Though dangerous to larger animals, it much more often attacks small prey.

Boto

Boto

The boto is the largest kind of amazon river dolphin. When the amazon floods, the waters cover many of the trees. The boto has no problem moving its way through the flooded forest. It can bend its body in amazing ways to move around. The long snout comes in handy for checking the river bed for food or darting through branches for small fish. Males can become a distinct pink color. This pink color makes them attractive to female botos.

Tortoise

Tortoise

18 islands off the coast Ecuador make up the Galapagos Islands. The islands were named after their giant tortoise. It is the largest tortoise in the world with some reaching over 5 feet in length and weiging 550 pounds! They can also live to over 100 years old. They have very easy lives, grazing on grass and other plant life, and sleeping around 16 hours a day. They digest very slowly and have water storage inside them, so they can go a year without eating or drinking if needed.
